### Excerpt 3

> "@metawarp7446 Prosthetics aren't integrated into the nervous system, so you don't get the fine autonomous control over your joints or the same range of motion, or any of the other things our meat limbs generally do (for example, I have all my meat limbs, but osteoarthritis means I don't have all the cartilage in my knees I should, and my extensor tenosynovitis in my right wrist means it's prone to painful inflammation, but they're still much more functional than any prosthetic). That's why you need different prosthetics for different purposes; a walking leg is better for, y'know, general walking, but it doesn't have the energy return of a running blade, let alone what our tendons do in our meat legs."
