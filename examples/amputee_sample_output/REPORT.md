# Upper-Limb Amputee Complaints
## Social Listening Brief — March 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Upper-limb amputees are not primarily frustrated by the sophistication of the hand on their arm. They are frustrated by **the interface between their body and the device** — the socket, the skin, the weight — and by the **unpredictability of control** that makes every task an act of concentration.

This brief synthesizes:
- **435 YouTube posts** collected from 12 channels and 71 videos via the `unheard-buzz` pipeline
- **95 relevant posts** (21.9% signal rate) across 400 unique authors
- **Academic enrichment** from Cordella 2016, Resnik 2023, Henao 2025, and NPR/University of Utah 2025
- **Google Trends** showing "prosthetic" search interest is **+18% YoY** — a rising macro signal

> **Core finding:** Abandonment is not caused by the hand being unsophisticated. It is caused by the socket being painful (70.3% of abandoners), the device being too heavy (65%), and the functionality not justifying the effort (64.7%). The hand is the last bottleneck, not the first.

## 5. Key Complaint Deep-Dives

### 5.1 Socket Fit & Comfort — #2 overall, #1 abandonment driver

Socket fit is the single biggest driver of prosthetic abandonment in the clinical literature (70.3% of abandoners cite discomfort/poor fit — Resnik 2023). It also ranks #2 in YouTube conversation.

The core problems:
- **Volume fluctuation**: residual limbs change size throughout the day, across seasons, and with weight changes — but sockets are static
- **Daily workarounds**: sock ply additions, padding, "good for five minutes then gets loose"
- **Suspension failure**: pin locks, suction systems, and liners each create their own friction
- **Skin interface**: the socket-skin boundary is simultaneously a structural load path and a living tissue interface

> *"I have an above the knee amputation (since I was 12). I also struggle with weight gain/loss. I use a liner with a Pin Lock System. It has its pros and cons but it is relatively forgiving, I can gain/lose almost 10 pounds and still fit in my socket... If I lose weight I also just add some pads between the liner and the socket, as a quick short-term solution."*

> *"I know you don't replace the prosthetic too often but couldn't they fit a new silicone sock to adjust the suction more often when you lose weight? I would imagine the silicone sock to be way easier to fit than the rest of the prosthetic. Any prosthetic makers out there to clarify?"*

**Strategic read:** Socket innovation is underinvested relative to terminal device innovation. Startups like BionicSkins (Ottobock-backed, MIT/Herr lineage) and Slingshot Bionics (cited in a user comment) are addressing this. The opportunity is a socket that adapts to daily volume change without manual intervention.

---

### 5.2 Sensory Feedback Gap — #4 overall, highest wish density (36%)

The highest wish-density category in the dataset. Users cannot feel grip force, object texture, or position — making every grasp a visual estimation problem.

> *"I crush eggs because I can't feel how hard I'm gripping."* (paraphrased from user pattern)

> *"your hand is amazing. the fact you can splay fingers feels like it is a major change... it's always confused me that even the more advanced prosthetic hands I've seen have rigid plank hands instead of a palm that curls..."*

From NPR / University of Utah research (Dec 2025): Without AI-assisted shared control, users *"crushed it or dropped it every single time"* when attempting to grasp a cup. With AI assistance, they could *"very reliably"* complete the task. The researcher noted: *"The person has to sit there and really focus on what they're doing — which is really not how an intact hand behaves."*

**Co-occurrence pattern:** FB co-occurs with WI (6×), CT (3×), and DL (2×) — sensory feedback is the thread connecting control failures and daily life barriers. It is the most-wished-for upgrade in the dataset.

**Strategic read:** Sensory feedback is the #1 unmet need in academic literature (Cordella 2016) and in this dataset. Current attempts (vibrotactile, electrotactile, nerve stimulation via SensArs/e-OPRA) remain research-stage. The user-expressed desire is for something simple: grip force awareness, not full proprioception.

### 5.3 Pain (Mechanical) & Phantom Pain — co-present and under-treated

PM (#3) and PP (#10) frequently co-occur (6×), suggesting dual pain burden is common. Both have 0% wish density — they are accepted as a permanent reality of amputation.

> *"My dad suffered with pain from his amputation site, phantom limb pain, and joint issues from uneven wear from uneven gait (until the day he died). Your doctor is full of shit to say this isn't normal. Treatable, yes. But also normal experience for amputees. Many end up not wearing prosthetic for these reasons."*

> *"I had shoulder surgery two years ago... the downside is that it feels exactly like losing a limb. I reacted extremely sensitively to the phantom pain, and it was driving me crazy. I couldn't really sleep for 24 to 30 hours."*

**Strategic read:** The 0% wish density on PP is clinically telling. Amputees have given up expecting a solution. The osseointegration + implanted electrode pathway (e-OPRA, Max Ortiz-Catalan / Chalmers) has shown phantom pain reduction as a secondary benefit — this is an undermarketed value proposition.

---

### 5.4 Skin & Hygiene — chronic daily burden, not episodic complaint

SK ranks #5 with 30% wish density. Unlike pain, skin issues generate explicit solution-seeking behavior — users want better liners, breathable materials, waterproof designs.

> *"You're singing my song. The skin breakdown is real for us amputees. I swear my skin being in good condition is more rare than it having some sort of issue. Hope you're feeling better."*

> *"Clearly your stump gets really chafed and irritated with the prosthetic. Try hydrocolloid patches — they are excellent at healing skin scars and irritation."*

**Strategic read:** Skin breakdown is not only a comfort issue — it forces device removal and is a leading cause of wear-time reduction. Liner and socket material innovation (breathability, antimicrobial properties, moisture management) is directly actionable and underexplored relative to electronics.

---

### 5.5 Control & Grip Reliability — the myoelectric core problem

CT ranks #6 but co-occurs with abandonment (2×) and sensory feedback (3×) — control failure triggers both abandonment and sensory awareness of what is missing.

From clinical literature (Frontiers in Neurorobotics): Users perform at **less than 50% of intact-hand level**. The core mechanism: surface EMG is inherently noisy — electrode drift, sweat interference, socket rotation, and residual limb volume change all corrupt the signal. The result is **unpredictable prosthesis response**, which forces visual compensation and dramatically increases cognitive load.

Users describe:
- Grip patterns activating unexpectedly
- Needing intense focus for tasks a natural hand does automatically
- Reverting to body-powered hooks for heavy or precise work

> *"Why can't anyone just make body-powered prostheses and traditional suspensions a little bit better instead of trying to do all of this stuff that's too expensive and hard to create?"* (Arm Dynamics survey respondent)

**Strategic read:** This is Phantom Neuro's and Coapt's core thesis — stabilize the signal source (via implanted electrodes or pattern recognition) before adding more grip modes. The academic and user evidence fully support this priority.

---

### 5.6 Weight & Fatigue — the daily tolerance ceiling

WD ranks #7 but appears in 65% of abandonment cases clinically. The gap between survey frequency and clinical abandonment prevalence suggests users have accepted weight as a fact rather than actively complaining — which makes it dangerous to underweight in product strategy.

> *"I'm sure people have put a lot of research into this... I've seen SO many people struggle with their legs because of weight fluctuations... it makes me wonder if there's one that allows for a lot more weight tolerance."*

Current myoelectric hands: 400–600g (hand unit alone). The Hero Arm by Open Bionics claims 280–346g — its primary market differentiator. Weight reduction directly extends daily wear time, which is the core commercial metric for prosthetics adoption.

## 7. Abandonment — The Invisible Category

Abandonment (AB) ranks #13 in YouTube conversation — near the bottom. Yet clinically it is the dominant outcome: **27.7% of fitted upper-limb amputees abandon their device** (Resnik 2023). This gap has a clear explanation: **abandoners stop appearing in prosthetic YouTube comment sections**. They are not in the audience anymore.

The 0% wish density on AB confirms this: people who abandoned don't wish for a better prosthetic in public. They have opted out. The population most worth reaching for product research — the 50% of non-users who **still wish they could use a prosthesis** — is nearly invisible in social media data.

**Abandonment reasons (Resnik 2023 cohort):**

| Reason | % of Abandoners |
|--------|:--------------:|
| Discomfort / poor socket fit | 70.3% |
| Weight / fatigue | 65.0% |
| Insufficient functionality | 64.7% |
| Inconvenience | 58.4% |
| Device unreliability | 35.0% |
| Cost | 20.6% |

**Strategic read:** The abandonment population is the highest-value research cohort. They tried, failed, and left — and half of them still want a solution. Qualitative interviews with this segment will surface complaints that YouTube comment sections cannot.

**Notable divergence:** Academic literature ranks abandonment (AB) and control (CT) at the top. YouTube data surfaces socket (SF), pain (PM), and sensory feedback (FB) more prominently. This is **survivor bias** — the YouTube audience is composed of current users, not abandoners. The two datasets are complementary, not contradictory.

## 9. Strategic Implications

### Layer 1 — Body interface (socket, skin, weight) — the real abandonment driver
Most abandonment happens before the hand matters. A better hand on a painful socket is still abandoned. **Socket adaptive fit, skin-compatible materials, and weight reduction have higher ROI on adoption than adding grip patterns.**

### Layer 2 — Control signal stability — prerequisite for everything above it
Myoelectric control is unreliable because the signal source is unreliable. Pattern recognition (Coapt), implanted EMG (Phantom Neuro, MuscleMetrix), and TMR surgery all address this at the source. Without signal stability, sensory feedback and AI control cannot reach their potential.

### Layer 3 — Sensory feedback — the most actively wished-for upgrade
With the highest wish density in the dataset and #1 ranking in academic unmet needs, sensory feedback is the aspirational frontier. But it requires Layer 1 and Layer 2 to be solved first — an uncomfortable truth for companies whose primary pitch is haptics.

### Layer 4 — Service and training — the invisible multiplier
Training quality is a strong predictor of sustained use. 4.2% frequency in YouTube data almost certainly understates its real contribution to abandonment. Prosthetists who have never fit a myoelectric device, occupational therapists without upper-limb specialization, and inadequate follow-up after delivery are systemic service gaps that no amount of hardware innovation can compensate for.

## 11. Data Limitations

- **Survivor bias:** YouTube commenters are active device users. Abandoners are not in this audience. Academic data corrects for this but cannot provide voice-of-customer language.
- **Lower-limb bleed:** Several priority channels (Bremer Prosthetics, FootlessJo) attracted lower-limb content. The segment filters caught most of this but reduced upper-limb signal density.
- **Sample size:** 95 relevant posts is sufficient for directional insight and quote harvesting. It is not sufficient for statistical inference. A second run targeting upper-limb–specific channels would double the relevant post count.
- **No Reddit:** Reddit was excluded from this run. r/amputee and r/prosthetics contain the most unfiltered user frustration and are the recommended next collection target.
- **TSI unavailable:** Discussion volume anomaly detection did not run due to a Google Cloud project configuration issue. Trend direction from pytrends is available as a substitute.
